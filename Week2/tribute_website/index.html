"Magic is the art of making things happen, at the right time, in the right place, for the right reason." – FitzChivalry Farseer, Assassin's Apprentice

Biography

Robin Hobb, born Margaret Astrid Lindholm Ogden in 1952, is a renowned American fantasy author best known for her *Farseer Trilogy*. Writing under the pseudonym Robin Hobb, she has captivated readers with her deeply emotional and character-driven narratives. Raised in Alaska, Hobb began her writing career under the name Megan Lindholm, producing works like *The Limbreth Gate* before adopting the Hobb persona for epic fantasy.

The *Farseer Trilogy*, beginning with *Assassin's Apprentice* (1995), introduced readers to FitzChivalry Farseer, a royal bastard trained as an assassin in the Six Duchies. Hobb’s ability to weave intricate plots with profound character development has made her a cornerstone of modern fantasy. Her works explore themes of loyalty, sacrifice, and identity, resonating with readers worldwide.

Beyond *Farseer*, Hobb expanded the Realm of the Elderlings with series like *The Liveship Traders* and *The Tawny Man*. Her contributions to fantasy include richly detailed worlds and empathetic portrayals of complex characters, earning her accolades and a dedicated fanbase.
